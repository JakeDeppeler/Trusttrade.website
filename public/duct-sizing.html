Ducted Designer — what it can & can't do
A quick guide for anyone using this tool.

What it does

  • Upload a plan, set the scale, draw rooms (box or custom shape) and drop ceiling outlets.
  • Works out kW per room, the design load (with diversity) and the airflow (L/s) needed.
  • Auto-design: picks a unit, splits into zones, places take-offs, runs the flex and sizes every duct.
  • Zone rooms — whole-home / day-night / per-room / custom, or name + zone each room as you draw it (own / group / on the main).
  • Builds a materials list + customer quote (unit, flex, take-offs, plenums, dampers, grilles, labour).
  • By hand: D draws a run, B drops a branch, drag flex/boxes to reshape. ⌘Z undoes.

Good to know

  • Rooms snap to square, straight walls; drag a box for a rectangle, or click each corner for an odd shape (click the first dot to close).
  • Take-offs try to stay within ~6 m (one bag of flex) of the outlets they feed.
  • "No zone / on the main" rooms are still conditioned but get no damper.

Limits

  • This is a design & take-off tool, not certified engineering — sense-check against AS 4254 / manufacturer data before you quote or install.
  • No AI plan-reading yet — you trace the rooms yourself.
  • Auto-design keeps ≤3 ducts off any point and avoids crossings, but on a busy plan it can't always guarantee zero.
  • Moving a duct re-solves the runs, so other runs can shift.
  • Your work lives only in this browser — use Save to keep a job (nothing is shared between people or devices).
Always check the numbers. Treat the output as a fast first pass, not a signed-off design.
